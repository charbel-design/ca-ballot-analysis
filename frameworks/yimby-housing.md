## Skeleton (to be filled)

### Overview

A housing-first framework weights housing supply and production more heavily than most other concerns, on the theory that California's housing crisis is the root cause of most other affordability, homelessness, equity, and economic-mobility problems, and that solving it benefits everyone.

### Character gate

Likely gate conditions:
- History of opposing housing supply (by-right development, ADU legalization, density near transit)
- Endorsement from homeowner associations that oppose housing
- Voting record of consistently blocking housing projects
- (Others to be specified)

### Weighted criteria (suggested structure, weights to be determined)

- **Housing supply record** (40-50% — dominant criterion)
- **CEQA and permitting reform** (20% — adjacent to supply)
- **Zoning and density positions** (15%)
- **Transit and infrastructure alignment** (10%)
- **Feasibility of implementation** (10%)
- **Other criteria** (5% — everything else combined)

### Expected picks

A YIMBY framework applied to CA ballots should reliably produce:
- Scott Wiener-aligned candidates where available
- Candidates endorsed by California YIMBY
- Candidates who supported SB 9, SB 10, SB 423, etc.
- YES on growth boundaries that force infill (infill >> sprawl)
- NO on parcel taxes that entrench incumbent homeowners
- NO on rent control (supply-reducing)
- YES on ADU and missing-middle-housing measures

Note: YIMBY framework can lead to unusual cross-partisan picks because housing positions don't map cleanly to left-right. Some progressives are strong YIMBYs; some are strong NIMBYs. Same for conservatives.
