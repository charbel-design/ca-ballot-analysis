# Worked Example: June 2, 2026 Primary — CA-10 / San Ramon / NPP Voter

## Scenario

Voter: San Ramon, CA 94582, registered NPP, Contra Costa County, under Prop 50 redistricting is in CA-10 and AD-16. Framework: fairness-not-equity.

This example shows a full workflow from Phase 1 (scope) through Phase 5 (deliverables). Use as a reference when handling similar primary ballots.

## Phase 1: Scope

**Election**: June 2, 2026 Primary (top-two, nonpartisan for most offices)
**Ballots mailed**: May 4
**Registration deadline**: May 18
**Election Day**: June 2

**Voter's ballot includes**:
- Statewide: Governor, Lt. Governor, AG, SoS, Controller, Treasurer, Insurance Comm, Supt. Public Instruction, BOE D2
- Federal: US House CA-10
- State: Assembly AD-16
- County: Assessor, Auditor-Controller, Clerk-Recorder, Treasurer-Tax Collector, Supt. of Schools, Superior Court Judge (one contested)
- Measures: Measure B (0.625% sales tax), Urban Limit Line renewal

**NOT on voter's ballot** (flag explicitly):
- State Senate SD-7 (odd-numbered, next up 2028)
- Board of Supervisors District 2 (voter's district, not up this cycle)
- SRVUSD school board (November only)
- San Ramon city offices (November only)
- DA and Sheriff (2028 presidential primary)

## Phase 3: Research (key findings)

### Character gate failures

- **Chad Bianco (Governor)**: Oath Keepers ties, ordered 2025 ballot seizure, worst crime-solving rate in CA. Pattern of rule-of-law violations.
- **Tom Steyer (Governor)**: Plan to criminally prosecute federal ICE agents. Rule-of-law violation.
- **Katherine Piccinini (CA-10)**: Religious-war framing as core message. Unseriousness.
- **Angela Griffiths (CA-10)**: Functional medicine practitioner. Pseudoscience red flag for federal office.

### Key scoring inputs (governor as example)

**Matt Mahan (D)**: San Jose mayor. 100% homicide clearance, cut impact fees, unblocked 2,000 housing units. Framework scoring: Delivery 85, Policy×Feasibility (85×70)/100 = 60, Five-year 80, Concentrated 75, Individual/Rule 75, Competence 85. Composite ~81.

**Antonio Villaraigosa (D)**: Former LA mayor and Assembly Speaker. Balanced LA budgets, cut violent crime 48%, raised graduation 60%. Framework scoring: Delivery 85, Policy×Feasibility (70×75)/100 = 53, Five-year 65, Concentrated 60, Individual/Rule 70, Competence 85. Composite ~69.

**Steve Hilton (R)**: Fox host, new entrant. Strong policy platform (zero state income tax for 7.1M, CEQA gut) but as Republican in D-supermajority state, feasibility near zero. Framework scoring: Delivery 40, Policy×Feasibility (90×20)/100 = 18, Five-year 75, Concentrated 80, Individual/Rule 85, Competence 70. Composite ~61.

Note: Hilton scored highest on raw policy quality, but the feasibility multiplier was decisive. This is the key calibration moment of the whole analysis.

### Measure B (sales tax) findings

- Court-ordered revision because original language was argumentative
- 2020 Measure X pattern (similar framing, 0.5% rate)
- $100K initial PAC funding from public employee union (IFPTE Local 21)
- General fund, not legally restricted to healthcare
- $150M × 5 years = $750M covers only fraction of projected $1B deficit by 2031
- Sales tax is regressive

### Urban Limit Line findings

- Extends 1990 Measure C / 2006 Measure L through 2051
- 65/35 preservation standard (65% of land open space)
- Voter approval required for expansion over 30 acres
- Updates criteria to include fire hazard zones, sea-level rise, steep slopes
- 25-year lock-in with modest Byron Airport carve-out

## Lessons / Patterns from this example

### What worked

- Character gate produced 4 clean disqualifications without controversy
- Feasibility multiplier decisively resolved the Hilton-vs-Mahan question (highlighted a common voter mistake of confusing "best policies" with "best pick")
- Explicit "what's NOT on the ballot" list prevented confused follow-ups
- County-level research caught the court-ordered Measure B language revision (a detail that's missing from most voter guides)

### What to improve next time

- Two picks shifted during conversation (Governor: Hilton → Mahan, SPI: Shaw → Muratsuchi) — this suggests the initial scoring was rushed. Slowing Phase 4 would have caught these up front.
- BOE D2 pick (Shireman) remained Low confidence throughout. For future low-leverage races, consider whether abstention is the better recommendation than a low-confidence pick.

### Framework calibration observations

The fairness-not-equity framework in this ballot produced:
- Mostly R picks statewide (7 of 9)
- Split picks downballot (2R, 2D, 2 nonpartisan)
- Clear measure votes (NO on tax, YES on growth boundary)

This distribution is consistent with the framework's stated priors (rule of law, individualism, fiscal discipline, anti-concentrated-benefit). A voter should NOT interpret this as "the framework is biased R" — it's specifically that this framework, applied to this 2026 California ballot field, produces this mix. A progressive framework applied to the same ballot would produce a different mix.

If your framework produces a substantially different distribution than this on the same ballot, your framework is working differently — that's fine, just be honest about which framework you're using.
